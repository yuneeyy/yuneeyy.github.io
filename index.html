01

Visual Refresh Across Touchpoints

Modernized FootfallCam's visual direction while keeping the original brand recognition. The refresh focused on sharper brand color, lighter logo weight, website UI, SaaS visuals, sales decks, and product presentation.

  • Refined brand color from muted grey-blue to a sharper business blue.
  • Adjusted logo weight for a lighter, more modern feel.
  • Applied the visual direction across website, SaaS screens, sales, and product visuals.
Process note

This was not a full logo redesign. The larger effort was modernizing the brand expression around the existing logo and making the website feel clearer, sharper, and more aligned with AI, IoT, and data analytics positioning.

02

Website Component Library and Workflow

Built a practical Figma component library to support faster page production and more consistent frontend handoff.

  • Defined breakpoints, typography, color styles, and reusable page structures.
  • Shared Figma files with frontend and discussed feasibility for new components.
  • Adjusted designs when interactions or components required too much development time.

500+ pages shipped across two years through a practical Figma-based workflow.

Handoff note

The workflow was practical rather than textbook. Components were built gradually as new pages were created, because the team did not know all required components upfront. My junior helped export images while frontend built from the shared Figma file.

03

Dashboard UI Foundation and Structure

Defined the early UI foundation for FootfallCam's analytics dashboard after management saw the improvement from the website refresh.

  • Explored dashboard visual direction and page hierarchy.
  • Created basic reusable components and layout structure.
  • Set a cleaner foundation for the project to continue.
Project boundary

My involvement focused on foundation work rather than the full product design cycle. After the initial direction was created, the project was continued by a junior designer.

04

Technical and Pitch Graphics

Created visuals that helped the team explain technical ideas clearly in sales meetings, partner presentations, webinars, documents, and internal discussions.

  • Connection diagrams, isometric graphics, and product hero images.
  • Pitch deck visuals, webinar slides, document templates, and brochures.
  • Storyboard direction and graphic assets for video projects.

For high-stakes presentations, I was often trusted to turn complex ideas into clear visuals.

05

Product Aesthetic and Physical Touchpoints

Supported hardware product development from the visual and user-facing side, helping new products stay aligned with the FootfallCam product family.

  • Explored product look and feel based on size, components, materials, heat, and signal constraints.
  • Created dimension drawings, packaging, user guides, and installation direction.
  • Supported unboxing video direction and portable exhibition booth concepts.
Role boundary

I was not the mechanical engineer. My contribution was product aesthetic direction, visual prototyping, documentation, packaging, and user-facing touchpoint design.
